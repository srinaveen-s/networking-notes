# VLAN (Virtual Local Area Network)

> **Status:** ✅ Completed

---

## 📖 Overview

A VLAN (Virtual Local Area Network) is a logical division of a physical network. It allows multiple groups of devices to communicate as separate networks even when they are connected to the same physical switch.

VLANs improve network performance, security, and management.

---

## 🎯 Why Do We Need VLAN?

Without VLANs, every device connected to a switch belongs to the same broadcast domain.

Using VLANs helps to:

- Reduce broadcast traffic.
- Improve network security.
- Separate different departments.
- Simplify network management.
- Increase overall network efficiency.

---

## 🌐 VLAN IDs

Some common VLAN IDs are:

VLAN 10 - HR Department

VLAN 20 - Finance Department

VLAN 30 - IT Department

---

## 🏢 Real-World Example

In an office, all employees may be connected to the same switch.

Using VLANs:

- HR computers communicate within VLAN 10.
- Finance computers communicate within VLAN 20.
- IT computers communicate within VLAN 30.

Even though they share the same switch, they operate as separate networks.
